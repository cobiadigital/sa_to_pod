Figure 6.A 1916 campaign flier promoting Sidney J. Catts for governor of Florida. Catts used virulent anti-Catholicism, populist rhetoric, and dry sentiment to become the only governor ever elected under the Prohibition Party banner. Though he was a Democrat, he embodied the budding links between anti-Catholicism and prohibition that bore their full fruit in the election of 1928 with the crushing defeat of Al Smith for president.
